Chandragiri

Chandragiri Hills is a popular tourist destination in Nepal, located to the southwest of the Kathmandu Valley. It is known for its panoramic views of the Himalayas and the Kathmandu Valley, making it a favorite spot for both locals and tourists alike.
Chandragiri Hills is situated about 7 kilometers from Thankot, which is a town on the western outskirts of Kathmandu, the capital city of Nepal.
The most common way to reach Chandragiri Hills is by road. Visitors can take a private vehicle, a taxi, or a local bus from Kathmandu to Thankot and then proceed to Chandragiri Hills. There is also a cable car service available from Thankot to Chandragiri, which offers a thrilling ride and beautiful views.
The Chandragiri Hills Cable Car is a major attraction in itself. It takes visitors from the base station in Thankot to the top of Chandragiri Hills, offering breathtaking views of the lush hills, villages, and the Kathmandu Valley below.
At the top of Chandragiri Hills, there is a viewpoint offering stunning panoramic vistas of the Himalayan range, including peaks like Mount Everest, Langtang, Ganesh, and Annapurna on a clear day. The view is especially captivating during sunrise and sunset.
There is a sacred Hindu temple called Bhaleshwor Mahadev at the hilltop, making it a popular pilgrimage site for both locals and devotees from neighboring regions.
Chandragiri Hills has restaurants and cafes where visitors can enjoy local and international cuisine while taking in the scenic views. Additionally, there are souvenir shops and rest areas for tourists.
For adventure enthusiasts, Chandragiri Hills offers hiking and nature walks to explore the surrounding area.
As with many tourist attractions in Nepal, there is an entry fee to access Chandragiri Hills, and separate fees may apply for the cable car ride.
Please note that the information provided here is based on the situation as of September 2021. It's always a good idea to check for updated information regarding entry fees, facilities, and any other changes that might have occurred since then.
